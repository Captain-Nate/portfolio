Sheen

▶ Playable
Puzzle game · bubble shooterPlayable on device

A bubble shooter with two ways to play — twenty handcrafted picture levels to clear shot-by-shot, and an endless mode where the ceiling keeps descending.

  • Hex-grid matching: flood-fill pops plus an anchor pass that drops severed clusters
  • Twelve themes and four bubble finishes, bought with coins earned from play or unlocked outright
  • Level mode is pure skill — pick every bubble colour you fire, so a loss is never bad luck

Under the hood Every palette is verified for colour-blindness before it ships: a solver simulates deuteranopia and protanopia, then maximises the worst-case perceptual distance between any two bubbles — one theme had greens a twentieth of the required separation apart, unplayable for roughly one man in twenty. The twenty levels are rasterised from real geometry rather than hand-placed, and a keystone detector rejects any board a single lucky shot could collapse.

Neon Drop

▶ Playable
Puzzle gameIn development

A neon-styled falling-block puzzle — rotate, drop, and clear lines to earn spendable “VOLTS” you cash in on themes and clear-burst effects.

  • 7-bag randomizer, ghost piece, hold slot and a 3-deep next queue
  • Combo-aware scoring with level and speed ramps
  • VOLTS economy + shop: score becomes currency for cosmetics
  • 3D neon presentation (URP bloom), haptics, screen-shake juice

Under the hood A pure, deterministic C# game core with zero engine references — so the exact same logic runs in Unity, under 23 automated unit tests, and in a headless balancing bot. Built twice: a fast JS/canvas prototype (the one you can play here), then a full 3D Unity port, parity-verified.

About

I’m Nate — I build and ship apps and games for iOS and the web. I take an idea from an empty project to a released product: design, architecture, native integrations, and the App Store paperwork. Six games and apps in the last few weeks.

I leverage AI to accelerate development — directing Claude Code alongside Xcode and Unity — without cutting the corners that matter: game loops that never stutter, one codebase that runs on both iOS and the web, in-app purchases and restore flows that actually work, and interfaces that respect the platform.

When there’s no off-the-shelf path for something — an iCloud sync layer, a WidgetKit widget, a Dynamic Island Live Activity — I build it. I’m looking for a role, full-time or contract, where I can own a game or a feature end to end.
